The problem

The human override has no oversight.

Every tool audits the AI algorithm. Nobody audits the human manager who overrides it.

When a manager rejects a candidate based on where they studied, vague cultural fit, or assumptions about physical capability — that decision disappears. No record. No challenge. No consequence.

Under the EU AI Act, that is a compliance violation. When enforcement begins, you have nothing to show the regulator.

EU AI ACT · ARTICLE 6 · HIGH-RISK AI SYSTEMS

AI systems used for recruitment are classified as high-risk. Organisations must maintain technical documentation and a detailed audit log of all AI-assisted decisions. Deadline: August 2, 2026.

Process

Four steps to compliance.

From rejection to audit-ready documentation in under two minutes.

01
Structured Input

Manager selects from a defined rejection taxonomy tied to job-relevant criteria only. No free text.

02
AI Analysis

NLP engine classifies the reason for proxy bias signals — institution bias, demographic proxies, vague cultural criteria.

03
Flag & Escalate

High-risk reasons trigger a structured justification or mandatory HR escalation before the decision is logged.

04
Audit Trail

Every decision is timestamped, logged, and exportable as EU AI Act compliant documentation automatically.
